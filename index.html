Nikola Tesla

    Tesla's inventions and patents

  • Born in 10 July 1856
  • Tesla was a Serbian-American inventor and engineer who contributed to the design of the modern alternating current (AC) electricity supply system.
  • He patented an induction motor that ran on AC, a Tesla coil, a wireless lighting system, and a radio-controlled boat.
  • He also experimented with X-rays, wireless power transmission, and global communications.

    • Tesla's projects and challenges

  • Tesla worked for Edison Machine Works, Westinghouse Electric, and his own companies, but often faced financial difficulties and competition from other inventors.
  • He built a large coil in Colorado Springs to test his wireless power and communication ideas, but failed to find investors for his ambitious plans.
  • He also built a tower in Wardenclyffe, New York, to transmit power and signals across the Atlantic, but it was never completed and was demolished in 1917.

    • Tesla's recognition and legacy

  • Tesla received several honors and awards, including the Edison Medal in 1917 and the unit of magnetic flux density named after him in 1960.
  • He was involved in several patent disputes and lawsuits, some of which were resolved in his favor after his death.
  • He died in New York City in 1943, leaving behind many unpublished notes and unfinished projects. His work inspired many later developments in science and technology.
